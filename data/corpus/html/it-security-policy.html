1. Purpose and Scope

IT Security Policy describes the formal policy requirements and operating procedures used by Northstar Analytics Ltd.. It is written as a synthetic but realistic document for training retrieval, summarization, and question-answering systems.

This document applies to all employees, interns, temporary workers, and approved contractors unless a section explicitly states otherwise.

Managers are responsible for enforcing this policy consistently and for escalating unusual cases to the document owner.

2. Roles and Responsibilities

Employees must read, understand, and follow the policy requirements relevant to their role.

Managers must document exceptions in writing and retain approvals in the designated system of record.

The document owner reviews this policy at least annually and whenever business, legal, or technical requirements materially change.

3. Identity And Access

The section on identity and access defines the standard rule, the approval path, and the records that must be retained.

Requests related to identity and access should be submitted through the official workflow rather than handled informally through chat or email alone.

When a manager grants an exception for identity and access, the exception must include the reason, the duration, and any compensating controls.

Production system access must be provisioned through role-based groups and approved by both the manager and system owner.

Shared accounts are prohibited except for approved service accounts with documented owners.

7. Incident Reporting

The section on incident reporting defines the standard rule, the approval path, and the records that must be retained.

Requests related to incident reporting should be submitted through the official workflow rather than handled informally through chat or email alone.

When a manager grants an exception for incident reporting, the exception must include the reason, the duration, and any compensating controls.

Security incidents must be reported to the Security team within 24 hours of discovery, and high-severity incidents should be reported immediately.

Suspected phishing messages should be reported using the mailbox reporting button and must not be forwarded externally.

8. Exceptions and Escalations

Exceptions must be rare, time-bound, and approved by the designated authority named in the relevant section.

All exceptions must be logged with the approver, rationale, expiration date, and any required follow-up actions.
