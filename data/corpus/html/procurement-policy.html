Procurement Policy

Owner: Finance
Version: 3.0-rag
Effective date: 2026-04-20

1. Purpose and Scope

Procurement Policy describes the formal policy requirements and operating procedures used by Northstar Analytics Ltd.. It is written as a synthetic but realistic document for training retrieval, summarization, and question-answering systems.

This document applies to all employees, interns, temporary workers, and approved contractors unless a section explicitly states otherwise.

Managers are responsible for enforcing this policy consistently and for escalating unusual cases to the document owner.

2. Roles and Responsibilities

Employees must read, understand, and follow the policy requirements relevant to their role.

Managers must document exceptions in writing and retain approvals in the designated system of record.

The document owner reviews this policy at least annually and whenever business, legal, or technical requirements materially change.

3. Vendor Selection

The section on vendor selection defines the standard rule, the approval path, and the records that must be retained.

Requests related to vendor selection should be submitted through the official workflow rather than handled informally through chat or email alone.

When a manager grants an exception for vendor selection, the exception must include the reason, the duration, and any compensating controls.

Records related to vendor selection must be stored in the designated repository with a clear owner and retention period.

Metrics related to vendor selection should be reviewed periodically to identify recurring issues and opportunities for improvement.

4. Three-Quote Rule

The section on three-quote rule defines the standard rule, the approval path, and the records that must be retained.

Requests related to three-quote rule should be submitted through the official workflow rather than handled informally through chat or email alone.

When a manager grants an exception for three-quote rule, the exception must include the reason, the duration, and any compensating controls.

Purchases above USD 5,000 normally require at least three competitive quotes unless a documented sole-source exception is approved.

Procurement must retain quote comparisons and award rationale.
