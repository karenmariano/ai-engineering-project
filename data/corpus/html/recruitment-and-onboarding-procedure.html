Recruitment and Onboarding Procedure

Owner: People Operations
Version: 3.0-rag
Effective date: 2026-04-20

1. Purpose and Scope

Recruitment and Onboarding Procedure describes the formal policy requirements and operating procedures used by Northstar Analytics Ltd.. It is written as a synthetic but realistic document for training retrieval, summarization, and question-answering systems.

This document applies to all employees, interns, temporary workers, and approved contractors unless a section explicitly states otherwise.

Managers are responsible for enforcing this policy consistently and for escalating unusual cases to the document owner.

2. Roles and Responsibilities

Employees must read, understand, and follow the policy requirements relevant to their role.

Managers must document exceptions in writing and retain approvals in the designated system of record.

The document owner reviews this policy at least annually and whenever business, legal, or technical requirements materially change.

3. Job Requisition

The section on job requisition defines the standard rule, the approval path, and the records that must be retained.

Requests related to job requisition should be submitted through the official workflow rather than handled informally through chat or email alone.

When a manager grants an exception for job requisition, the exception must include the reason, the duration, and any compensating controls.

A new role cannot be posted until the job requisition includes an approved budget code, job description, and hiring justification.

Backfills for already budgeted roles still require headcount confirmation from Finance.

5. Offer Approval

The section on offer approval defines the standard rule, the approval path, and the records that must be retained.

Requests related to offer approval should be submitted through the official workflow rather than handled informally through chat or email alone.

Offer letters must be approved by the hiring manager, People Operations, and Finance when compensation is outside the benchmark band.

No verbal promise of compensation or start date is binding until the signed offer letter is issued.

7. 30-60-90 Onboarding

The section on 30-60-90 onboarding defines the standard rule, the approval path, and the records that must be retained.

Requests related to 30-60-90 onboarding should be submitted through the official workflow rather than handled informally through chat or email alone.

When a manager grants an exception for 30-60-90 onboarding, the exception must include the reason, the duration, and any compensating controls.

Managers must complete a documented 30-60-90 day plan for every new employee by the employee's first week.

The plan should include role outcomes, stakeholder introductions, and required training milestones.

8. Exceptions and Escalations

Exceptions must be rare, time-bound, and approved by the designated authority named in the relevant section.

All exceptions must be logged with the approver, rationale, expiration date, and any required follow-up actions.
