Code of Conduct

Owner: Compliance
Version: 3.0-rag
Effective date: 2026-04-20

1. Purpose and Scope

Code of Conduct describes the formal policy requirements and operating procedures used by Northstar Analytics Ltd.. It is written as a synthetic but realistic document for training retrieval, summarization, and question-answering systems.

This document applies to all employees, interns, temporary workers, and approved contractors unless a section explicitly states otherwise.

Managers are responsible for enforcing this policy consistently and for escalating unusual cases to the document owner.

2. Roles and Responsibilities

Employees must read, understand, and follow the policy requirements relevant to their role.

Managers must document exceptions in writing and retain approvals in the designated system of record.

The document owner reviews this policy at least annually and whenever business, legal, or technical requirements materially change.

3. Ethical Conduct

The section on ethical conduct defines the standard rule, the approval path, and the records that must be retained.

Requests related to ethical conduct should be submitted through the official workflow rather than handled informally through chat or email alone.

When a manager grants an exception for ethical conduct, the exception must include the reason, the duration, and any compensating controls.

Records related to ethical conduct must be stored in the designated repository with a clear owner and retention period.

Metrics related to ethical conduct should be reviewed periodically to identify recurring issues and opportunities for improvement.
