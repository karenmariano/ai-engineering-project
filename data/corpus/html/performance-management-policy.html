Performance Management Policy

Owner: People Operations
Version: 3.0-rag
Effective date: 2026-04-20

1. Purpose and Scope

Performance Management Policy describes the formal policy requirements and operating procedures used by Northstar Analytics Ltd.. It is written as a synthetic but realistic document for training retrieval, summarization, and question-answering systems.

This document applies to all employees, interns, temporary workers, and approved contractors unless a section explicitly states otherwise.

Managers are responsible for enforcing this policy consistently and for escalating unusual cases to the document owner.

2. Roles and Responsibilities

Employees must read, understand, and follow the policy requirements relevant to their role.

Managers must document exceptions in writing and retain approvals in the designated system of record.

The document owner reviews this policy at least annually and whenever business, legal, or technical requirements materially change.

3. Goal Setting

The section on goal setting defines the standard rule, the approval path, and the records that must be retained.

Requests related to goal setting should be submitted through the official workflow rather than handled informally through chat or email alone.

When a manager grants an exception for goal setting, the exception must include the reason, the duration, and any compensating controls.

Records related to goal setting must be stored in the designated repository with a clear owner and retention period.

Metrics related to goal setting should be reviewed periodically to identify recurring issues and opportunities for improvement.

5. Rating Definitions

The section on rating definitions defines the standard rule, the approval path, and the records that must be retained.

A rating of 'Exceeds Expectations' indicates performance that consistently surpasses core role requirements with measurable impact.

A rating of 'Needs Improvement' triggers a structured review conversation and may lead to a performance improvement plan.
